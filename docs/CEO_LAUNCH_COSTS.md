## $SLS Token — Utility, Not Security

### Howey Test Results

| Prong | Satisfied? | Why |
|-------|-----------|-----|
| 1. Investment of money | **NO** | Users pay for a subscription — SLS is the consumption unit within that subscription |
| 2. Common enterprise | **NO** | No pooled funds, no profit sharing, no DAO |
| 3. Expectation of profit | **NO** | SLS is consumed (spent) during anchoring — not held for appreciation |
| 4. Efforts of others | **NO** | Users consume SLS for immediate utility, not investment returns |

**$SLS fails all four prongs.** It is a utility token — like a cloud compute credit or postage stamp.

### What This Means for Costs

| What We DON'T Need | Estimated Savings |
|---------------------|-------------------|
| SEC Reg D / Reg CF filing | $15K–$50K saved |
| Blue Sky (state securities) registration | $10K–$30K saved |
| Securities counsel retainer | $20K–$50K/year saved |
| Ongoing SEC reporting | $15K–$25K/year saved |

| What We DO Need | Cost |
|-----------------|------|
| Utility token legal opinion (one-time) | $5K–$15K |
| Ongoing monitoring of SEC/FinCEN guidance | Minimal (internal) |

**Net regulatory cost: $5K–$15K one-time** vs. $60K–$155K+ if it were a security.
