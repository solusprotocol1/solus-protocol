Who We Are

Solus Protocol is an early-stage blockchain startup headquartered in Mount Pleasant, South Carolina. Founded by a solo developer with deep expertise in blockchain technology and healthcare data challenges, we are creating a decentralized protocol on the XRP Ledger to set a new standard for secure, patient-controlled medical data integrity.

Our focus is real-world utility: immutable hash anchoring for medical records, zero-knowledge privacy (PHI never touches the chain), and seamless integration with existing EHR systems ‚Äî all built with transparency, compliance, and long-term scalability in mind.

Our Mission

Our mission is to empower patients with true ownership and control over their medical data while enabling healthcare providers to access accurate, verifiable records in a compliant, efficient, and tamper-proof manner ‚Äî beginning with a focused rollout in South Carolina to create meaningful local economic and health impact before expanding nationally.

We believe the future of healthcare depends on trust in data. Solus Protocol exists to make that trust immutable, decentralized, and accessible to everyone.

Why "Solus"?

We chose 'Solus' from the Latin word meaning 'alone' or 'only' because our protocol empowers patients to have sole control over the integrity of their medical data. In a fragmented healthcare system full of intermediaries, Solus creates a single, unbreakable source of truth on the XRPL ‚Äî unique, independent, and patient-centered. It's not about storing data; it's about making each record's authenticity stand alone, verifiable by anyone, forever.

Our Impact

By solving the problems of fragmented and insecure healthcare data, Solus Protocol aims to significantly reduce the estimated $300 billion annual cost of data mismanagement in the US healthcare system. In South Carolina, we are prioritizing partnerships and pilots with institutions like the Medical University of South Carolina (MUSC), which serves over 2.4 million patients statewide, to drive innovation, improve care coordination, and create lasting economic value in the region.

Long-term, our goal is to become the global standard for decentralized medical data integrity, enabling safer, faster, and more equitable healthcare worldwide.

SDK Integration

How providers can use the Solus Protocol prototype SDK

Getting Started with the SDK

The Solus SDK is a Python library that allows providers to integrate secure hashing and XRPL anchoring into their EHR systems or standalone applications. The prototype is available on GitHub and demonstrates core functionality.

Installation: pip install cryptography xrpl-py

GitHub Repo: View SDK Prototype

Key Features for Providers

  • Generate SHA-256 hashes of records for immutability.
  • Optional AES encryption for sensitive data (off-chain).
  • Anchor hashes on XRPL using $SLS utility token (micro-fees & rebates).
  • Fiat mode for USD payments (crypto abstracted).
  • Verify record integrity across providers.

Prototype Code Example

from solus_sdk import SolusSDK

# Initialize SDK with testnet
sdk = SolusSDK(api_key="your-api-key", testnet=True)

# Secure a patient record with explicit type
result = sdk.secure_patient_record(
    "Patient vitals: BP 120/80, HR 72",
    wallet_seed="sYourWalletSeed",
    record_type="VITALS"  # Categorizes for metrics
)
print(result)  # Returns hash + XRPL tx confirmation

Real transactions anchored to XRPL testnet. View live data on our Metrics Dashboard.

Verification Example

# Verify a record by comparing hashes
original_record = "Patient vitals: BP 120/80, HR 72"
stored_hash = result['hash']  # From secure_patient_record()

# Recompute hash to verify integrity
import hashlib
computed = hashlib.sha256(original_record.encode()).hexdigest()
print(f"Verified: {computed == stored_hash}")  # True

Security & Safety

Your data is safe, secure, and tamper-proof with Solus Protocol

Security Assurance

Solus Protocol is designed with privacy-by-design: Protected Health Information (PHI) is never stored on-chain. Only cryptographic hashes are anchored on the XRP Ledger, ensuring immutability while keeping sensitive data in provider-controlled, encrypted storage.

The protocol is tamper-proof: Any alteration to a record changes its hash, instantly detectable. XRPL's institutional-grade consensus provides high security and reliability.

Compliance & Safety

We support HIPAA alignment through off-chain PHI handling and encryption. Full audits and certifications planned post-pilot.

Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about Solus Protocol, $SLS token, and healthcare data security

What is Solus Protocol?+
Solus Protocol is a decentralized healthcare data integrity platform built on the XRP Ledger. We enable patients and providers to securely hash, verify, and share medical records without exposing Protected Health Information (PHI) on-chain.
Is patient data stored on the blockchain?+
No. Only cryptographic hashes of medical records are stored on the XRP Ledger. The actual patient data (PHI) never touches the blockchain and remains securely stored off-chain in HIPAA-compliant systems. This ensures privacy while enabling tamper-proof verification.
What is the $SLS token and how is it used?+
$SLS is a utility token on the XRP Ledger that powers the Solus Protocol ecosystem. It's used to pay micro-fees for anchoring record hashes, earn rebates for participation, and will eventually be used for governance. The token is non-speculative and designed purely for protocol utility.
Where can I trade $SLS?+
$SLS is live on the XRP Ledger mainnet and can be traded on xMagnetic, xpmarket, Xaman Wallet, and the Sologenic DEX. Visit our Roadmap section for direct links to all trading platforms.
Is Solus Protocol HIPAA compliant?+
Solus Protocol is designed with HIPAA compliance in mind. Since no PHI is stored on-chain (only cryptographic hashes), the protocol maintains a zero-knowledge approach to patient data. Full HIPAA compliance certification is part of our Phase 3 roadmap milestone.
Why build on the XRP Ledger?+
XRPL offers extremely low transaction costs (~$0.001), fast confirmation times (3-5 seconds), energy efficiency, and enterprise-grade reliability. These characteristics make it ideal for healthcare applications that require frequent, low-cost transactions at scale.
How can healthcare providers integrate with Solus Protocol?+
We're developing SDK plugins for popular EHR systems like Epic and Cerner. During our pilot phase, we're working directly with healthcare providers in South Carolina. Visit our Contact section to inquire about partnership opportunities.
What are Cross-Chain Bridges?+
Cross-chain bridges will allow $SLS to be used across multiple blockchain networks beyond XRPL. This increases accessibility, liquidity, and enables healthcare organizations on different blockchain ecosystems to participate in Solus Protocol. Learn more at solusprotocol.com.
How is Solus Protocol funded?+
Solus Protocol is currently self-funded and actively seeking strategic partnerships, grants, and investment. We're applying to healthcare innovation grants and engaging with angel investors and VCs focused on healthtech and blockchain. Contact us to learn about investment opportunities.
What is the total supply of $SLS tokens?+
$SLS has a fixed supply designed for long-term protocol sustainability. Token distribution includes allocations for ecosystem development, team vesting, and community incentives. Full tokenomics details are available in our whitepaper.
What makes Solus different from other healthcare blockchain projects?+
Unlike competitors that store PHI on-chain, Solus uses a zero-knowledge approach where only cryptographic hashes are stored. This ensures privacy compliance while enabling verification. We're also built on XRPL for ultra-low costs (~$0.001/tx) vs Ethereum's $5-50 per transaction, making us viable for high-volume healthcare use cases.
