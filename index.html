Frequently Asked Questions

Does Solus store my medical records on the blockchain? +
No. Solus Protocol is built on a "Privacy-by-Design" principle. We only store a cryptographic SHA-256 hash (a digital fingerprint) on the XRP Ledger. Your actual medical records remain in the secure, HIPAA-compliant databases of your healthcare provider.
Why was the XRP Ledger (XRPL) chosen? +
The XRPL was chosen for its institutional-grade reliability, carbon neutrality, high transaction speeds, and extremely low costs. Its built-in DEX and AMM capabilities also provide immediate liquidity for the $SLS utility token.
Is $SLS a security? +
The SLS token is a utility-based digital asset designed for data anchoring and technical governance within the Solus ecosystem. It represents no ownership or equity in any company. Users should review our Legal Notice regarding global regulatory risks.
How can developers integrate Solus? +
We are launching a production-ready Enterprise API and SDK in Q4 2026. This will allow EHR (Electronic Health Record) developers to integrate automated hash-anchoring into their existing software workflows.

PROTOCOL ARCHITECTURE: HASH ANCHORING

Privacy-by-Design

Solus remains HIPAA/GDPR compliant by never storing Protected Health Information (PHI) on-chain.

Cryptographic Fingerprinting

Uses SHA-256 hashes to create irreversible "fingerprints" of medical records while data stays off-chain.

Integrity Controls

The XRPL ensures that once a hash is anchored, it is immutable and time-stamped for auditability.
